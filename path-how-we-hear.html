How We Hear

Hearing involves complex processes in the outer, middle, and inner ear, converting sound waves into electrical signals interpreted by the brain.

Types of Hearing Loss

  • Sensorineural hearing loss: Damage to hair cells in the inner ear or the auditory nerve (e.g., age-related).
  • Conductive hearing loss: Sounds unable to pass from outer to inner ear due to blockage.

Common Conditions

Conditions include Meniere’s Disease, Otosclerosis, and age-related loss (Presbyacusis).
