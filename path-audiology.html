Understanding Hearing Loss

Hearing loss is generally categorized into two main types:

  • Sensorineural: Caused by damage to the sensitive hair cells inside the inner ear, or damage to the auditory nerve. This is commonly seen as 'age-related' hearing loss.
  • Conductive: Occurs when sounds are unable to pass from the outer ear to the inner ear, often caused by a blockage inside the middle ear.

Hearing Aid Maintenance

Knowing how to keep a hearing aid working could be the difference between comfortable hearing and social isolation. Essential maintenance includes:

  • Changing the batteries regularly.
  • Keeping the earmoulds clear of wax and debris.
  • Retubing earmoulds when they become hard or discolored.
  • Replacing thintubes as advised by your audiologist.
