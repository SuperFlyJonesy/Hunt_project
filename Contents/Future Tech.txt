Future Tech Tile: Auracast & Next-Gen Accessibility
Here is the up-to-date breakdown for your Future Tech tile on Auracast, highlighting the world-first trial at Bristol Temple Meads, local Bristol organizations, and the wider venue rollout across the UK.

1. What is Auracast?
Auracast™ broadcast audio is a new standard built on Bluetooth® LE Audio (using the LC3 codec). Unlike traditional Bluetooth, which restricts connections to a single pair of devices, Auracast allows a single transmitter to broadcast high-quality, low-latency audio to an unlimited number of receivers simultaneously without needing to pair.

Direct-to-Ear Streaming: Sound streams straight to compatible hearing aids, cochlear implants, or earbuds—bypassing background acoustic noise and reverberation.

Zero Complex Equipment: Users tune in via a smartphone app (or dedicated pocket receivers for legacy devices) much like selecting a Wi-Fi network.

Battery Efficient: Uses a fraction of the power of classic Bluetooth, preserving hearing aid battery life throughout the day.

2. Bristol Temple Meads Deployment (World-First Trial)
Bristol Temple Meads is at the global forefront of accessible transport technology, serving as the world's first public rail station trial of Auracast.

The Technology: Installed by Ampetronic (in partnership with Listen Technologies), deploying their Auri™ system—a network of 10 weather- and pigeon-proof transmitters mounted in relay across high-traffic zones, including Platforms 9–12, the pedestrian subway, and the main Ticket Hall.

The Innovation Zone: Funded through Connected Places Catapult and Network Rail as part of the Station Innovation Zone at Temple Meads to evaluate how next-gen assistive listening reduces travel anxiety and replaces outdated induction loops.

Real-World Impact: Station PA announcements, delays, and safety alerts stream in real-time straight to passengers' ears with clear audio and near-zero latency (~30 ms).

3. Local Bristol Organizations & Key Partners
Centre for Deaf and Hard of Hearing People (CfD Bristol): Based at the Vassall Centre in Fishponds, Bristol. CfD actively supports local residents with hearing loss and participated in trial evaluations to ensure user needs are reflected in accessibility tech.

Connected Places Catapult (Bristol Station Innovation Zone): The UK’s transport innovation hub leading trial deployments at Temple Meads alongside Innovate UK.

Royal National Institute for Deaf People (RNID): Partnered with trial developers to conduct in-depth user feedback studies on-site at Temple Meads.

4. UK Venue Adoption & 2030 Roadmap
Beyond transport hubs, major cultural and public venues across the UK have committed to adopting Auracast infrastructure:

Confirmed Venue Deployments & Trials: Manchester’s Contact Theatre, The National Theatre, Shakespeare’s Globe, Southbank Centre, the Bridge Theatre (London), and Birmingham Hippodrome.

Industry Pledge: UK venue operators, hearing care professionals, and tech providers have publicly committed to achieving widespread Auracast integration across arts, healthcare, and retail spaces.
