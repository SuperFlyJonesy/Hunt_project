Real experiences and stories shared by hard-of-hearing residents across Bristol, showing the power of connection, comfort, and assistive technology.

“

Meeting up through the local hearing support group changed everything for me. You finally realize you aren't the only one who spends all evening nodding along when you haven't actually caught a word.

— Marcus Vance

“

Getting the RIC style fitted properly took away that awful occluded feeling. It feels like a natural part of me now rather than an intrusive plastic gadget in my ear.

— Chloe Bennett

Standing on a busy platform and hearing the gate change announcement crystal clear through my streamer without straining? Absolute game changer for travel.

“

Trying to figure out the local device pathways on your own is completely overwhelming, but having a clear, step-by-step roadmap made sorting my options so much less stressful.

— Fiona Gallagher

“

For years I couldn't hear a single word from anyone sitting on my left side in a crowded pub. The CROS system finally bridges that gap and brings me back into the conversation.

— David Miller

“

Hearing loss is such an invisible, lonely road until someone sits down across from you with a cup of tea and just gets how utterly exhausting it is to keep up all day.

— Elena Rostova

“

Knowing I can request replacement tubing easily by post without jumping through hoops removes that low-level daily anxiety of a cracked line failing on me.

— Arthur Pendelton

“

All the flashy smartphone apps and AI features in the world don't mean a thing if the physical device pinches or whistles. Comfort and fit have to come first.

— Samantha Cole

“

Hearing my grandchildren whispering secrets in the back seat of the car for the very first time brought actual tears to my eyes. I didn't realize how much I was missing.

— Robert Taylor

“

It’s not just about technical amplification; it’s about having a supportive space where people understand the reality of living with hearing loss and lift each other up.

— Karen Hughes

The Power of Shared Experiences

Hearing loss can feel like a solitary journey, but you are never alone. Over 62,220 adults in Bristol navigate similar paths every single day. Whether you are finding the right physical fit for a receiver-in-canal (RIC) device, experimenting with cutting-edge assistive technology like Auracast, or simply sharing a warm cup of tea with someone who truly understands, every step forward is easier when taken together.

Our initiative aims to bridge the gap between clinical audiology and human connection, offering clear roadmaps, support groups, and a network that advocates for accessibility in every public venue across our city.
