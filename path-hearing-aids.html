Hearing Aids

Types

  • BTE (Behind-the-Ear): Common type, held by earmould or thintube.
  • ITE (In-the-Ear): Sits inside the outer ear.
  • ITC (In-the-Canal): Sits farther in the canal.
  • Body Worn: Less common, for profound loss. Worn by very elderly or dementia patients; microphone and receivers far apart reduce feedback.

Components & Maintenance

Loop (via programme button): Use to access induction loops.

  • Earmoulds/Thintubes: Require regular cleaning and replacement.
  • Maintenance: Change batteries, clear earmoulds, retube as needed.
